Harbourvale City Television - a history

Much has been said about the history of this City. In fact, the history for the airing of Harbourvale's Television spans back to 1964 (in Singapore retrospective)!

1964 - Televysion Blakton Harbourvale Pura

In 1963 Singapore aired Televysien Singapura, So Harbourvale City aired about a year after Singapore aired TV as Televysien Blackton Harbourvale Pura. It Aired with 2 Channels - Channel 6 and Channel 9.Channel 6 is a English TV Channel like Channel 5 Channel 9 is a Mandrin TV Channel like Channel 8

1965 - Radio Television Harbourvale

When Both Singapore & Harbourvale was kicked out in 1965 Singapore gained independence and Harbourvale was on it's own so Harbourvale Gained independence in 12 August TV Blackton Harbourvale Pura was Changed to RTH or Radio Television Harbouvale.

1980 - Harbourvale Brodcasting Corporation

RTH Ceased Transmission on 31 Jan 1980 like RTS (Radio Television Singapore) into HBC. In 1983 the logo for HBC was introduced it was an extended logo version of the SBC from the S Becoming a H.

1989 - Harbourvale Brodcasting Corporation 25 Years of Television

HBC Celebrates it's 25th year of brodcasting since 1964 HBC changed its wallpaper looks to fit in with the animation wallpaper seen in the opening & Closedown.

1994 - Television Corporation of Harbourvale

HBC became tch which was Television Corporation of Harbourvale HBC Channel 6 became just Channel 6 like HBC Channel 5 became Channel 5 and HBC Channel 9 became Channel 9 of dijouboadao.

2001 - MediaHorp

tch became MediaHorp in 2001 as tcs became MediaCorp in the same year

2001 - 2005 - HPH Mediaworks

HPH(Harbourvale Press Holdings) Mediaworks was founded in 2001 with 2 channels - Channel V and Channel J. Channel V is the 2nd Mandrin Channel in Harbourvale while Channel J is also the 2nd English Channel in Harbourvale. When HPH MV said that they will merge with MediaHorp in New Years Day of 2005, Channel V's Licence was given to MediaHorp While Channel J Ceased Transmission on 1 Jan 2005.

2005 - MediaHorp

After the Merger of HPH Channel 9 became from the diboadao into pingdao, to fit in with Channel V Vpingdao. In 2008 MediaHorp finds HPH's Channel J Frequency Signals to form mokto. Mokto is okto, the M stand for Microsoft.
